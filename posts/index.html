Dark Matter

One of the most definitive (and up-to-date) overviews of Dark Matter comes from the Particle Data Group’s Review of Particle Physics. A web page similar to what I wrote below, but with pictures, is by physicist Tim Linden. Do we know Dark Matter exists? For most physicists today the answer is a pretty straightforward “yes.” In physics there is a general philosophy that we should look for the simplest explanations to what we see in nature, and Dark Matter is the simplest explanation in this case.

Meet Lisp

Lisp is a special language among the giants (Java, C, Python) in that it’s a functional language: everything in the language is done by a call to a function. For example, (+ 2 3) is how you would add 2 and 3. Of course you can define a function, and this itself involves a call to the function defun (defun myfunction (v1 v2) (* v1 v2)) The code above defines a function that takes two variables as input and returns the product of them.

Programming: what is passing?

In Python, so-called primitive types are passed by value. >>> x = 5 >>> x 5 >>> y = x >>> y 5 >>> y = 7 >>> y 7 >>> x 5 Other types are passed by reference. This means that the memory location of an object is passed, and modifications (even to a different variable name that’s pointing to the same location) affect that one object.

Orthodox Books

Here’s a listing of books I’ve read concerning some aspect of the Orthodox Church. This list will be added to, and modified, as needed. Chadwick, Henry. East and West: The Making of a Rift in the Church. Oxford University Press, 2005. Henry Chadwick was a recognized authority on the history of the early Church. He was an Anglican, which probably lends some author neutrality to this study of the origins and causes of the division between the East and West.

Spiritual Books

Similar to Orthodox Books, this is a listing of spiritual books that I have come across and found useful. This list will be added to or modified as needed. Bloom, Anthony. Beginning to Pray. Paulist Press, 1970. Described on the back cover as a “modern spiritual classic,” Archbishop Anthony’s book describes prayer from a simple and honest perspective. Bloom has a refreshing approach to common spiritual obstacles, opting for stories from personal experience and unique comparisons rather than repeating some well-known Church teaching.
