Ode to Agamemnon

February 9, 2019

Agamemnon: 2015 – 2018

Our kitty of 3 years, Agamemnon, passed away yesterday after a sudden onset of acute illness. To those who knew him, Agamemnon was outgoing, obnoxious, curious, and devoted. He will be missed.

From the time he was adopted back in 2015, Agamemnon had a special attachment to Blaise, who was nearing his first birthday. If Blaise was upset in his crib, Agamemnon would often hop in to try to console him. Blaise loved to play toys with Kitty (or “Key!” as Blaise was first learning to talk), and Agamemnon absolutely enjoyed it. Agamemnon was extremely forgiving of Blaise’s awkward attemps to “love” him, allowing inadvertant rough treatment without much fuss. This forgiveness was not extended to other members of the family. Ann commented more than once that, “Blaise can do no wrong in his book.” This special connection continued through the rest of Agamemnon’s life.

Agamemnon’s bold personality manifested itself in diverse ways. He gained the nickname “terrorist” for his antics of repeatedly jumping at the bird’s cage; laying on his back inviting passers-by to pet his tummy, only to grab their arms with his paws and bite them; hiding behind curtains to ambush the next person to walk by. But there was no one that Agamemnon didn’t try to befriend; he immediately approached all new visitors, the desire to acquire a new friend overriding any shyness.

With such boldness, it was perhaps inevitable that gracefulness was not one of Agamemnon’s strong points. Objects were inadvertently knocked off counters and tables while he was on one of his climbing expeditions, to the great chagrin of certain family members. But his endearing personality always won the day; even Mark couldn’t stay angry after Agamemnon walked across his keyboard, or jumped on his lap insisting on snuggles and petting. Cecilia and Agamemnon often clashed in spectacular clashes of wills over whether the cat really wanted to play games just now, or whether it was really okay that the cat was sitting in that spot; the stubborness of each meant that these clashes were often a draw. Ann was Agamemnon’s primary caretaker, and they shared many snuggles together. Next to Blaise, Ann had the most devoted love for Agamemnon and took great amusement in his many quirky behaviors.

As he grew older, Agamemnon showed a devotion such that he was trusted to go outside, and he unfailingly showed up at the back door after getting his dose of fresh air. These outdoor excursions were notably shorter when no one else was outside with him; for a cat Agamemnon was very social and didn’t enjoy being away from his family for long. We grieve the loss of our dear Kitty, but receive some consolation in that he seemed to enjoy his life with us as much as we enjoyed him. Goodbye, Agamemnon.
